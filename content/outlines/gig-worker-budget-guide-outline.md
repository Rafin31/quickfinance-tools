## H2: Why Standard Budget Advice Fails Gig Workers
- Featured snippet target: "how to budget when income varies month to month" - numbered steps
- The 50/30/20 rule assumes a fixed income. Variable income needs a different foundation.
- Core problem: fixed expense commitments against variable income = dangerous mismatch

## H2: The Baseline Budget: Start With Your Floor
- HowTo schema steps:
  1. List all fixed monthly expenses (rent, utilities, subscriptions, debt minimums)
  2. Add variable necessities at their minimum (food, transport)
  3. Sum = your income floor - the minimum you must earn each month
  4. Set this as your client minimum / floor target
- Everything above the floor is allocatable

## H2: The Percentage Budget: A Better System for Variable Income
- Instead of fixed dollar amounts, allocate percentages of every payment received
- Example allocation: 30% taxes / 15% emergency fund / 10% savings goal / 45% living expenses
- Works whether you earned $2,000 or $8,000 this month
- External link: Federal Reserve Survey of Consumer Finances (income volatility data)

## H2: Setting Aside Taxes: The Non-Negotiable First Step

## H2: Building an Emergency Fund on Irregular Income
- Dual function: income smoothing buffer + true emergency fund
- 3-month floor = income smoothing layer (covers slow months)
- 3-6 months additional = true emergency layer
- Internal link: /blog/how-much-emergency-fund-freelancer ("how much emergency fund a freelancer needs")

## H2: Paying Yourself a "Salary" as a Gig Worker
- Buffer account strategy:
  - All client income -> business/buffer account
  - Transfer fixed monthly "salary" to personal checking
  - Eliminates lifestyle inflation on good months
  - Smooths income without touching emergency fund
