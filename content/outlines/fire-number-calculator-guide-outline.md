## H2: What Is a FIRE Number?
- Featured snippet: 40-60 word definition immediately after H2
- Formula: Annual expenses x 25 = FIRE number
- Example: $40,000/year spending -> $1,000,000 FIRE number

## H2: The 4% Rule: Where the 25x Formula Comes From
- Trinity Study (1994, William Bengen, Journal of Financial Planning)
- External link: Trinity Study / Bengen original research
- 30-year horizon caveat: early retirees need to consider longer timelines (40-50 years)
- Historical success rate: 95%+ for 30-year periods with 50/50 stock-bond portfolio

## H2: Lean FIRE, Fat FIRE, and Barista FIRE
- Table: Type / Annual spend / FIRE number / Who it suits
  - Lean FIRE: under $40K/year, FIRE $1M, frugal lifestyle
  - Fat FIRE: $100K+/year, FIRE $2.5M+, maintain current lifestyle
  - Barista FIRE: partial retirement + part-time, FIRE $500-800K

## H2: Coast FIRE: The Alternative for Freelancers
- Definition: enough invested that compound growth alone funds retirement without more contributions
- Table: "If you have $X invested at age Y, you can coast to retirement at 65" (using 7% real return)
  - Age 25: $128,000 -> coast to $1M at 65
  - Age 30: $184,000 -> coast to $1M at 65
  - Age 35: $265,000 -> coast to $1M at 65
  - Age 40: $383,000 -> coast to $1M at 65
- Works well for freelancers who want to reduce work, not eliminate it
- Internal link: /calculators/savings-goal ("savings goal calculator")

## H2: How Freelancers Should Approach FIRE Differently
- Variable income = sequence of returns risk matters more
- Freelancers already have flexible income - reduce clients vs full stop
- Healthcare costs pre-65 (no employer coverage): budget $600-$1,200/month
- Social Security: freelancers still pay in, still eligible

## H2: Common FIRE Calculation Mistakes
- Using nominal not real returns (7% real, 10% nominal - big difference over 30 years)
- Ignoring sequence of returns risk
- Not budgeting healthcare pre-65
- Forgetting inflation on expenses (3% annual = expenses double in 24 years)
