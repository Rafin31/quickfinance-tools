## H2: How Self-Employment Deductions Work (The Mechanism)
- Featured snippet: 40-60 word explanation
- Deductions reduce net SE income -> lowers SE tax (15.3%) AND income tax
- Critical insight: a $1,000 deduction saves $153 in SE tax PLUS income tax savings - most guides miss this

## H2: The 50% Self-Employment Tax Deduction
- The automatic deduction most articles bury
- IRS lets you deduct half of SE tax on Schedule 1 (above-the-line)
- Example: $9,180 SE tax -> $4,590 deduction automatically

## H2: Home Office Deduction: The Rules That Actually Apply
- Exclusive use test explained clearly (no "sometimes use this corner")
- Two methods:
  - Simplified: $5/sq ft, max 300 sq ft = max $1,500
  - Actual expenses: percentage of rent/mortgage + utilities + insurance
- Can I deduct home office if I rent? YES (cite IRS Pub 587)
- External link: IRS Publication 587

## H2: Vehicle and Mileage Deductions
- 2026 IRS standard mileage rate: 67 cents/mile
- Standard mileage vs actual expenses - when each makes sense
- Commuting is NOT deductible; client visits, supply runs ARE

## H2: Health Insurance Deduction for Self-Employed Workers
- Deducted on Schedule 1 (above-the-line, reduces AGI)
- Eligibility: cannot be covered by spouse's employer plan
- Covers premiums for you, spouse, dependents, children under 27
- External link: IRS Publication 535

## H2: Retirement Contributions: SEP-IRA, Solo 401(k), and SIMPLE IRA
- Table: Plan / 2026 limit / Deadline / Admin complexity
  - SEP-IRA: up to 25% of net SE income, max $69,000 (2026)
  - Solo 401(k): employee ($23,500) + employer (25% of net SE), combined max $69,000
  - SIMPLE IRA: $16,500 employee limit + 3% employer match
- Internal link: /calculators/savings-goal ("savings goal calculator") for SEP-IRA planning framing

## H2: Business Expense Deductions: What Qualifies and What Doesn't
- Table: Deductible vs Not Deductible
- Deductible: software, subscriptions, equipment, professional development, professional services, 50% of business meals
- Not deductible: personal clothes (even if worn to client meetings), gym memberships, personal meals
- External link: IRS Schedule SE
